# V2 Multi-Signal Filtered Maker Strategy

## Strategy Overview

The V2 strategy builds on the V1 Longshot YES Maker thesis — sell YES contracts at longshot prices (3-12¢) and profit when markets resolve NO — but adds multi-signal filtering based on empirical analysis of 72M+ Kalshi trades.

### Core Thesis (unchanged from V1)
- **Role**: MAKER (provide liquidity via limit orders)
- **Direction**: Sell YES at longshot prices
- **Edge source**: Retail takers systematically overpay for longshot outcomes (favorite-longshot bias)

### V2 Improvements
1. **Price filter**: Focus on 3-12¢ sweet spot (2.0-2.3% maker excess vs 0.6-1.2% at 1-3¢)
2. **Category filter**: Weight by proven maker-taker gap; exclude categories with no edge
3. **Time-of-day filter**: Evening hours (16-23 ET) have ~2x maker edge vs early morning
4. **Regime filter**: Rolling 500-trade window must show positive maker excess
5. **Start date**: Only trade post-2024 (maker edge only emerged mid-2024 per data)
6. **Risk management**: Fractional Kelly sizing, category exposure limits, collateral-aware position sizing
7. **Collateral lockup model**: Capital is locked when entering positions and only released at market resolution

## Data Analysis (72M+ Kalshi trades, 2021-2025)

### Key Findings

**Regime Shift**: Maker edge only emerged in mid-2024. Before Q4 2023, takers consistently won. This aligns with prediction markets maturing and retail volume increasing.

## Backtest Audit & Methodology Notes

### Bugs Found and Fixed
The initial backtest showed $10K -> $2.15M (21,427% return, 6.10 Sharpe). This was wildly overstated due to three bugs:

1. **Instant resolution (CRITICAL)**: Trades were resolved immediately, recycling capital infinitely. At peak, the model would have needed $8.77M in concurrent collateral but only had $10K (877x overstatement). **Fix**: Track open positions with resolve_time; lock collateral at entry, release at market close.

2. **Category exposure no-op (HIGH)**: `category_exposure[cat] = category_exposure.get(cat, 0)` never added trade collateral, so the 30% category cap never triggered. **Fix**: Track actual locked collateral per category.

3. **Sharpe inflation (MEDIUM)**: Days with zero P&L were dropped, inflating Sharpe by ~30%. **Fix**: Include all calendar days in Sharpe calculation.

### Remaining Assumptions
- **Fill rate is modeled, not observed**: The 5% base fill rate matches V1 observations but actual fills depend on order placement, market depth, and competition from other makers.
- **Lookahead in category weights**: Category weights were derived from the same dataset used for backtesting. In production, these should be periodically recalibrated.
- **Regime filter has ~39% lookahead**: Uses outcomes of positions not yet resolved. Impact is moderate — the regime filter is a safety net, not core alpha.
- **Resolution is certain**: We know the market result at trade time. This doesn't affect correctness (fills are random, not conditioned on outcome) but means we don't model mark-to-market P&L during the holding period.
- **Fees are now included**: Exact Kalshi maker fee formula applied per trade. Total fee drag is 4.7% of gross P&L.
